Mayor Adams wants a City of yes, but can the city hold itself together long enough to grow?

Recent analysis of NYC Housing Maintenance violations in the wake of the recent Bronx building collapse shows the scale of NYC's hidden housing safety crisis.

By Alex Corey

Dec. 14 2023

New York City has had many a joke made about it's housing conditions from the chides of shoe box apartment sizes,to tales of inhumanly unreasonable landlords these are all things newcomers will hear from family elsewhere before moving to the city. But the recent building collapse in the Bronx raises a few questions to those considering a move to the city. Namely, how can a building with 103 housing violations continue collecting rent without any governmental agency stepping in? According to a recent study of Housing and Preservation Department data, it seems to be a borough-specific issue. (See chart Below)

Approximately 80% of the Top 10 zip codes with the highest rate of maintenance violations are secluded within the two poorest boroughs in the city, Brooklyn and the Bronx.(See Chart below)

In response to the recent Bronx building collapse, Deputy Mayor Meera Joshi elaborated on how thinly spread resources led to the disaster "We're a city of millions of buildings and 500 something inspectors, so we will never have boots on the ground get to every building." Joshi further elaborated that without an escalating fine system tacked on to the city's efforts to crack down on unsafe apartments, things won't change any time soon.
